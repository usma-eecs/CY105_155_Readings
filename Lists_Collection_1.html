So far we have seen built-in types like int, float, bool, str and we’ve seen lists.  The types int, float, and bool are considered to be simple or primitive data types because their values are not composed of any smaller parts. They cannot be broken down. On the other hand, lists are different from the others because they are made up of smaller pieces.

Types that are comprised of smaller pieces are called collection data types. Depending on what we are doing, we may want to treat a collection data type as a single entity (the whole), or we may want to access its parts. This ambiguity is useful.

A list that contains no items, often referred to as the empty list, is still considered to be a list. It is simply a sequence of zero items and is represented by [] (two square brackets with nothing in between).
